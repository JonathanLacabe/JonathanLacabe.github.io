Summary of Multivariable Calculus

Prerequisites:

Math:
-Single Variable Calculus

Physics:
-Knowledge of Vectors is necessary. While basic concepts are introduced and explained within the pages themselves, it is recommended to obtain a full, unabridged understanding of vectors (as well as how they relate to different subjects) from the source material itself, Classical Mechanics Subsections III.I through III.II.

These are my complete notes for Multivariable Calculus, covering such topics as Vectors, Matrices, Partial Derivatives, Gradients, Lagrange Multipliers, Non-Independent Variables, Double & Triple Integrals, Vector Fields, Green's Theorem, Flux, Stokes' Theorem, and more.

I color-coded my notes according to their meaning - for a complete reference for each type of note, see here (also available in the sidebar). All of the knowledge present in these notes has been filtered through my personal explanations for them, the result of my attempts to understand and study them from my classes and online courses. In the unlikely event there are any egregious errors, contact me at jdlacabe@gmail.com.
